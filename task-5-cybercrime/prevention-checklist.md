UPI & Financial Safety

01. Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money
Your PIN is only required when you are sending a payment. If someone asks you to "confirm receipt" using your PIN, that is a scam — end the call immediately.

02. Set a daily UPI transaction limit
Most UPI apps let you cap how much can be transferred per day. Keep this limit low — it significantly reduces the damage if your account is ever compromised.


General Online Safety

03. Do not click links from unknown senders
Phishing links often arrive via SMS or WhatsApp, disguised as bank alerts, scholarship portals, or job offers. Go directly to the official website instead of tapping any link.

04. Use unique passwords for every account
Reusing one password across multiple apps means a single breach exposes everything. Use a password manager like Bitwarden (free) to maintain different credentials for each platform.

05. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
Turn on 2FA for your email, social media, and banking apps. Even if someone gets your password, they cannot log in without the second verification step.

06. Avoid public Wi-Fi for financial transactions
College canteens, hostels, and café networks are easy targets for interception. Always switch to mobile data before making any payment or logging into your bank account.

07. Do not share OTPs or screen access with anyone
Legitimate bank officials, app support teams, or government employees will never ask for your OTP or request remote access to your phone via apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer.

08. Verify before you trust — always double-check
If a classmate, seller, or "official" contacts you urgently asking for money or personal details, call them back on a number you already know. Urgency is one of the most common tactics scammers use to prevent you from thinking clearly.

If you've been scammed - report it immediately

National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930
Time matters — report within the first few hours to increase the chance of freezing the fraudulent transaction.
